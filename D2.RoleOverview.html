THE ROLE
What an Appointment Setter Actually Does
IN ONE SENTENCE
An Appointment Setter contacts potential residential customers to introduce solar and battery solutions, qualifies their suitability, and books them in for a consultation with a RESINC specialist — over the phone.
Your role is a mix of inbound and outbound calling. You're educating prospects, qualifying leads, handling objections, and representing RESINC's brand on every single call.
📞
Conduct outbound calls
Contact potential customers from your lead list — D2D reschedules, internet enquiries, and referrals.
☀️
Introduce solar clearly
Explain the basics of solar and battery in a way that's simple, engaging, and builds genuine curiosity.
✅
Qualify every prospect
Check interest, property suitability, and eligibility before booking. Quality appointments only.
🛡️
Handle objections confidently
Professionally and calmly address concerns — without being pushy or defensive.
📅
Book quality appointments
Schedule in-home or Zoom consultations for RESINC specialists. The appointment has to be real and qualified.
📝
Keep the CRM accurate
Record customer details, call outcomes, and notes correctly every time. If it's not logged, it didn't happen.
🎯
Hit your KPIs
Meet or exceed daily, weekly, and monthly appointment targets. Consistency beats motivation every time.
🤝
Represent the brand
Every call is a customer's first impression of RESINC. Your tone, your professionalism — that's the brand.
🔍
Identify how they found us
Ask how they heard about RESINC — and make the connection if it's through a partner or ambassador.
📈
Keep learning
Participate in every training session. The best setters are the most coachable ones — not the most experienced.
WHY THIS ROLE MATTERS
The Most Critical Step in the Sales Process
This is the customer's first personal interaction with RESINC. Whether they found us through a door knock, an ad, a referral, or a partner — this call is their first real experience of who we are.
1
You create qualified opportunities
You filter and qualify leads so RESINC's specialists spend their time with people who are genuinely interested and eligible. An unqualified appointment wastes everyone's time — including the customer's.
2
You increase sales efficiency
By handling the initial outreach and objections, you allow the sales team to focus entirely on closing — not prospecting. Your work directly determines how productive every specialist's day is.
3
You build trust before anyone else does
You introduce solar clearly and professionally, shaping the customer's first impression of RESINC. Without strong appointment setters, the sales team would struggle with low-quality leads, wasted time, and inconsistent results.
WHERE YOU FIT
You Are Stage 1
RESINC's customer journey has 8 stages — from first contact through to 25 years of ongoing care. You own Stage 1. But knowing what comes after makes you better at Stage 1.
1
Appointment Booking — This Is You
You contact prospects, qualify them, and book the consultation. The specialist, the installation, the 25-year support — none of it happens without a qualified appointment. Every booking you make is the start of a customer's journey with RESINC.
YOUR LEADS
Three Lead Sources — Know the Difference
Unlike some teams who work one lead type, your team handles three. Each one comes in with a different context — and needs a slightly different approach.
🚪
RESINC D2D Reschedules
A door knocker visited this customer and they booked — then rescheduled or cancelled. They're warm. They already said yes once. Your job is to re-engage and get them back in the calendar.
🌐
Internet Leads
The customer expressed interest online — through an ad, a form, or a website enquiry. They've raised their hand but haven't had a real conversation yet. Speed to contact matters here.
👥
Referrals
An existing RESINC customer or partner has recommended someone. These leads come in with the highest trust — someone they know has already vouched for RESINC. Handle with care and appreciation.
